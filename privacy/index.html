Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 19, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Claybreak ("the app," "we," "us") handles your information. Claybreak is a personal clay-target shooting journal that lets you log range sessions, rounds, equipment, and notes across trap, skeet, and sporting clays. The app is local-first: your journal lives on your device and works fully without an account.

By using the app, you agree to the practices described here.

Summary

  • The app works without an account. If you never sign in, your data never leaves your device.
  • We collect only what the app needs to function: your journal entries, and — if you choose — your location (once per session) and your account email.
  • We do not sell your data, show ads, or use third-party advertising or analytics trackers.
  • You can export your data at any time and delete your account and all cloud-stored data from within the app.

What the app stores on your device

When you use the app, the following is stored locally on your device in a private database:

  • Sessions and rounds — dates, disciplines, targets, hits, yardage, and scores
  • Notes — any tags or text you add
  • Equipment setups — gun, choke, shell/load, and notes you enter
  • Range information — names and approximate coordinates of ranges you confirm
  • Weather snapshots — conditions captured at the start of a session

This on-device data is under your control. Uninstalling the app removes it.

Location

If you grant location permission, the app reads your device location one time at the start of a session to help identify the shooting range you are at. Location is used only at that moment — the app does not track your location in the background and does not record a location history beyond the ranges you choose to save.

To turn your coordinates into a usable place name, your approximate coordinates are sent to:

  • OpenStreetMap / Nominatim — to look up nearby named venues
  • Open-Meteo — to fetch the weather conditions for that location

These services receive only the coordinates needed to answer the request. You can deny or revoke location permission at any time in your device settings; the app remains fully usable, and you can name ranges manually instead.

Accounts and cloud sync (optional)

Creating an account is optional and exists only to back up and sync your journal across devices. If you sign in, we use Supabase (our authentication and database provider) to:

  • Verify your identity using your email address, or a Google or Apple sign-in
  • Store a copy of your sessions, rounds, notes, and equipment setups so they can be restored on another device

Your cloud data is protected by access rules that allow only your account to read or write it. Data is transmitted over encrypted (HTTPS) connections.

If you sign in with Google or Apple, that provider authenticates you and shares a basic identifier and email with us; we do not receive your password.

What we do NOT do

  • We do not sell or rent your personal information.
  • We do not display advertising.
  • We do not include third-party advertising or analytics SDKs.
  • We do not track your location in the background.

Exporting your data

The app includes a CSV export feature that compiles your sessions, rounds, and notes into a file you can share or save through your device's standard share sheet. This file is generated on your device; where it goes after you share it is up to you.

Deleting your data

  • Local data: Uninstalling the app removes the on-device database.
  • Cloud data: If you created an account, you can permanently delete your account and all associated cloud-stored data from the Profile screen using Delete account. This action is immediate and cannot be undone. Data stored only on your device remains until you uninstall the app.

Children

The app is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from them.

Third-party services

The app relies on the following services, each governed by its own privacy policy:

  • Supabase — authentication and cloud database (only if you sign in)
  • Open-Meteo — weather data
  • OpenStreetMap / Nominatim — reverse geocoding for range names
  • Google and Apple — optional sign-in providers

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. Material changes will be reflected by updating the "Last updated" date above.
